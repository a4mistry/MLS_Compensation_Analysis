02 — The Superstars

The 1% of the pitch

MLS runs a barbell economy. A small elite of Designated Players sits at the top, a vast base earns near the minimum, and the middle is thin.

The top 10% of earners take home of all the money in the league. The single highest-paid player alone earns — enough to fund a full roster of league-minimum players several times over.

Guaranteed compensation includes base salary plus marketing and bonus money — the standard MLS pay yardstick.

03 — The Market by Role

Attack pays. Goalkeeping doesn't.

The transfer market prices creativity and goals. Attacking midfielders and wingers command the highest median pay; goalkeepers — however vital — are the cheapest specialists on the field.

Does spending buy wins?

Here is the question every owner asks. We plot each club's total payroll against its points-per-game this season. If money bought results, the dots would hug the trend line.
